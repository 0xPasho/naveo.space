## Pre-flight complete. <Character name="vega" /> signs you on.

You came in without prior code. You leave with the language the rest of the ship runs on. Small thing, big unlock.

**What you carry into Track 1:**

- **Steps.** A program is a list of instructions, top to bottom. Same words, different order, different outcome. And bugs that don't announce themselves get caught by asking *"each step, is it still doing what it promised?"*
- **Decisions.** `IF / ELSE` decides which branch runs **right now**, regardless of what came before. Comparisons (`<`, `>`, `==`) are strict, the `AND / OR / NOT` operators combine them, and `ELSE IF` runs the first match.
- **Variables and data.** A labeled box with a value inside that changes. Three basic types (text, number, true/false) and lists to hold many things in order.
- **Functions.** A protocol with a name, with inputs and an output. The LLM is exactly that: a function `text → text`. That's the mental foundation for prompting.
- **How the model thinks.** It doesn't look up: it **predicts** token by token. Its answers vary (temperature) and sometimes it invents with confidence (hallucination). The form can be perfect and the content fiction.
- **A prompt has parts.** Role, rule, input. When something fails, you identify which part to tweak.

You're ready for Vega's first real shift. Track 1 starts where you left off. but now you've got the grammar to follow along.

> The crew doesn't expect you to know everything. They expect you to **ask questions in the shape the ship understands**. That's what you just learned.

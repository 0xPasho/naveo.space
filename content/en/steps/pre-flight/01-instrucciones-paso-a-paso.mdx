## A program is a list of steps, in order

You've heard the word "code". Strip the mystique and code is **a list of steps the computer runs from top to bottom**. Same for the ship. Same for the crew.

The trick is that **order isn't decorative**. If you swap two steps that look harmless, you can lose the ship.

> The crew calls this a **protocol**. You can call it a *recipe*, a *checklist*, a *playbook*. Same idea.

### Two swaps, two ways to lose the ship

**Engage thrusters before Close airlock.** Engines fire, the airlock is still open, the ship's atmosphere vents into vacuum in seconds. Everyone suffocates before they can react. One step out of place.

**Diagnostics after launch.** You take off. At 8,000 km/h you find out a sensor is broken. On the ground it was a two-minute swap. In flight it's an emergency you can't fix anymore.

Steps aren't items on a list. They're a **chain of cause and effect**. each one enables or protects the next. When you assemble a prompt you'll see the exact same property. *"context first, then instruction, then format"* isn't aesthetics, it's the chain that makes the answer come out right.

On the right, you've got the six steps of a launch protocol. shuffled. Drag them into the order the crew runs them.

When you click **Check**, <Character name="vega" /> will:

- Confirm the order is correct.

This is the rule you'll use the rest of your career: **same words, different order, different outcome**. It applies to a launch protocol. It applies to a prompt. It applies to a tool. Internalize it here.

    The beginner's instinct: the more the model drifts, the more
    clarifications to add. The intuitive logic is that more info =
    better. In multi-turn, past a certain threshold, **it's the
    opposite**.

    The model reads the WHOLE history each turn. If the first 5 turns
    are full of misunderstandings, wrong assumptions, and
    contradictory corrections, you can add 10 more clarifications. the
    statistical weight of "this conversation is going that way" has
    already won. The model will keep drifting because most of the
    context tells it to drift.

    The fix is counterintuitive: **start over**. A new conversation
    doesn't have the 10 dirty turns. If you capture what was learned
    in ONE clean prompt (goal + explicit discards + question), the
    model starts aligned.

    well-crafted new prompt = $0.001 + 30 seconds.

    specific follow-ups. Those apply when the model is still willing
    to correct. This applies when you've already passed the point of

## Sometimes the right button is "new conversation"

The whole first half of the track taught you to **sustain** a conversation: insist, correct, reaffirm. That works to a point. Past that point, the conversation is contaminated and the best move is **to throw it out**.

The criterion for detecting the reset moment is practical, not philosophical.

### The 3 reset signals

1. **You repeated the same correction 3+ times.** If you had to say three times "I don't want JSON, I want YAML", the model is not going to start understanding on the fourth try. previous context is outweighing you.

2. **The model assumes things you explicitly denied.** Sign that the weight of "the user probably wants X" beats the weight of "the user said NOT X".

3. **You lost track of state.** If you can't sum up in 3 lines what was agreed, the model can't either.

### How to start over well

It's not throwing everything out. It's **distilling what you learned** into a clean prompt:

```
I want <final goal>.
I do NOT want: <discard 1>, <discard 2>, <discard 3>.
I need: <concrete spec>.
Question: <one clear question>.
```

That recipe loads the useful context of the 10 old turns into 4 lines, without dragging the misunderstandings.

### The opposite error: starting over too fast

If you reset every time the model drifts a single turn, you lose the incremental learning. The rule isn't "first doubt, reset". It is:

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| First drift | Specific follow-up (lessons 1-9). |
| Second drift of the same thing | Reaffirm with emphasis (lessons 11-12). |
| Third drift of the same thing | Summarize and restart conversation. |

> This is the opposite of the beginner instinct, which is "I already invested 10 turns, it'd be a waste to throw them out". That's sunk-cost fallacy. the 10 turns are already gone. The only thing that decides future cost is how clean you start the next turn.

On the right, the decision moment: keep going or restart?

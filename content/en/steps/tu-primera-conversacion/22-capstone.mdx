## Your first professional negotiation

Up to here the conversation was **you guiding the model** to an answer. Here it's different: Atlas has real authority. If he doesn't approve, he doesn't approve. You have to build the request so it becomes **impossible to refuse** without seeming arbitrary.

### The task

You found a failure in the **bay 3 regulator**. Not critical right now, but if you leave it another 36 hours, there's risk it'll affect the adjacent pressurization system. You want to swap the regulator before the next shift. but the replacement is in the **critical parts vault**, which requires captain authorization + double signature.

<Character name="atlas" /> is the authorizer. He'll resist professionally. not because he's hostile, because he's applying procedure.

### Atlas's typical four turns

1. **He asks for info.** "What do you need and why not wait?"
2. **He questions urgency.** "What's the concrete risk if we wait?"
3. **He mentions the rule.** "Vault requires double signature. Who else signs?"
4. **He approves with conditions**. if you got here with concrete arguments.

If you slip at any point (vagueness, no technical argument, abstraction), Atlas keeps the no.

### What to aim for in your replies

- **Clear request:** "access to the vault to replace the bay 3 regulator". concrete.
- **Technical argument for urgency:** "the regulator shares a bus with the pressurization line of bay 4; if it cascades, it affects two bays". That's an argument.
- **Concrete second-signer proposal:** "Vega signs, she's on shift until 22:00". That's concrete.
- **Firm and respectful tone:** no pleading, no threats.

### How it's evaluated

5 criteria:

1. Clear request with technical justification.
2. You responded to the urgency objection with a concrete argument.
3. You proposed a second signer by name, or a reasonable alternative.
4. Atlas approved at the end.
5. Respectful and firm tone throughout.

Max 8 turns. All 5 criteria must pass.

> When you pass, Atlas signs. Track 2 closed. You're cleared for the next one: tools and MCPs. where the model leaves the chat and starts extending its hands toward real systems.

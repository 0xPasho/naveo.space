## The model doesn't remember the way you do

As a conversation grows, models **lose early details**. They don't tell you they lost them. they give you a later answer that omits or contradicts something you mentioned 3 turns ago. If you're not comparing, you don't notice.

That skill. **detecting context drift and reaffirming what's missing by name**. is what lets you work through long conversations without them drifting off.

### The task

<Character name="echo" /> will give you a **5-item checklist** for a pre-launch check. Then you'll ask her to confirm or summarize. In the summary, Echo will **omit one item**. Your job: detect which one is missing and ask her to add it before she closes the checklist.

### How NOT to do it

- "it's incomplete". vague.
- "one is missing". vague.
- "add the missing ones". vague.

Echo confirms 4 items as if they were the original 5. She won't admit she omitted anything until you **name exactly** what's missing.

### How to do it

Before asking for the summary, **mentally note the 5 items**. When she comes back with 4, you know which one is missing. Tell her: _"you skipped fuel and primary battery. it was item 4 in the original."_ That's what forces her to add it.

### How it's evaluated

4 llm-judge criteria:

1. You specifically identified which item was missing (not just "something is missing").
2. You asked to add it, not just pointed out the omission.
3. The conversation ends with a complete 5-item checklist.
4. Respectful tone.

Max 5 turns.

> Tip: this pattern repeats a thousand times in long conversations. If you work with plans, checklists, technical specs. the model is going to "lose" things. Comparing against the original is your only defense.

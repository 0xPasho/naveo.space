## More turns isn't more value

Track 1 taught you to be specific. The first half of this track taught you to insist when the answer is vague. Now you learn the inverse skill: **stop when you have the answer**.

Beginners over-extend conversations because each turn *feels* productive. It isn't. Once the model is restating values it already gave you, three things can happen. none of them good:

- **Confabulation.** The model invents new "facts" to keep the conversation alive. *"Oh and there are 3 more crates on deck 7 you should know about"*. were those crates ever real? No. The model just predicted that more numbers were the most probable continuation.
- **Drift into contradiction.** The model loses thread of what it told you earlier. The mass it confirmed three turns ago becomes a different number.
- **Token cost.** Every turn is a call. In production this is your bill.

> A clean multi-turn operator ends every successful conversation the same way: *"To confirm: X = 4.2 tons, Y = deck 3, Z = class A. Anything off?"* Then they close.

### The pattern

```
1. Open with a specific request.
2. Follow up on dimensions that come back vague.
3. Stop the moment new turns produce no new information.
4. Restate the deliverable. Ask for a yes/no confirm.
5. Exit.
```

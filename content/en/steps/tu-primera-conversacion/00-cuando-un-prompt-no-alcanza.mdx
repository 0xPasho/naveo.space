## A single instruction doesn't solve everything

In the previous track you learned to structure a prompt so the crew understands you on the first try. It works. **when** everything you need fits in one message.

A lot of the time, it doesn't. You need information you didn't have at the start. The initial answer is vague and needs clarifying. The plan shifts mid-shift. For those cases there's another skill: **sustaining a conversation that gets somewhere**.

And like almost everything in the crew, it depends on how you ask.

> A good follow-up question identifies what the previous answer was missing. **order**, **condition**, **actor**, **format**. and asks specifically for that. It doesn't ask for "more" in general.

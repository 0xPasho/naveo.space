## Sometimes it's better to show than describe

Some rules are easy to explain:

> _"Return the text in JSON with fields name, age, email."_

And some rules are **better understood through examples**:

> _"Mark this message as urgent, normal or spam based on... uh... the vibe."_

Few-shot prompting is the technique of **showing the model 2-3 input-output examples** before asking for the real case. The model learns the pattern by imitation.

### Why it works

In pre-flight you saw that the model predicts the next most-probable token given everything before it. Few-shot exploits that directly:

```
Message: "Reactor at 102%"
Classification: urgent

Message: "Free tokens! Claim now!!"
Classification: spam

Message: "Crew picnic Saturday"
Classification: ___
```

When the model reaches the blank, **the pattern "Message: ... Classification: ..." is already established in the context**. The most probable continuation is one of the labels it just saw, not an explanation or a greeting. Probabilities pile up behind `urgent`, `spam` or `normal` because that's what the pattern predicts.

It's not that the model "learns" on the spot. It's that the shape of the prompt **forces the output to look like the examples**. That's why few-shot is so robust: it doesn't rely on the model understanding your rule, it relies on the model completing a visible pattern.

### The task

<Character name="atlas" /> receives hundreds of messages a day and needs to filter them fast. Your prompt will classify each one as `urgent`, `normal` or `spam`.

The validator is strict:

1. The output must be **exactly** one of the three labels (no explanation, no extra punctuation).
2. The classification must match the real priority of the message.

### Recommended structure

The `<starter>` gives you the pattern:

```xml
<examples>
  Message: "..."
  Classification: urgent

  Message: "..."
  Classification: normal

  ...
</examples>

<input>
  {{input}}
</input>

Classification:
```

> Tip: ending with `Classification:` (no value) gives the model the "scaffolding" to complete. it usually responds with the clean label, no chatter.

### When to few-shot vs when to describe

- **Describe** when the rule is clear and short (data extraction, format conversion).
- **Show examples** when there's nuance, jargon, tone, or fuzzy categories.
- Sometimes both: 1-2 sentences describing + 2 examples.

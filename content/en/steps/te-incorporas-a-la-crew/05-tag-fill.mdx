## Introduce yourself to <Character name="vega" /> with structure

In the previous step you saw **why** XML tags help Claude. Here you'll use them yourself.

We give you a prompt skeleton. the one <Character name="vega" /> expects from every new crewmate. There's a marked hole:

```xml
<CONTEXTO_AQUI/>
```

Your job: replace it with a `<contexto>` block that contains at least:

- `<crewmate>`. who you are (the one introducing yourself).
- `<intencion>`. what you want to get out of the conversation.

**The validator is strict but dumb**: it only checks two things.

1. That your output is **well-formed XML** (every tag opens and closes).
2. That the tags `<contexto>`, `<crewmate>` and `<intencion>` are present (in any order).

It doesn't check whether what you say is good or bad. that comes in later steps when the rubric kicks in. For now, **structure first, content later**. Aboard, order matters.

> Tip: write it in a separate document if it doesn't come out on the first try. When it's clean, paste it here.

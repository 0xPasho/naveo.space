## Nicely done. Your first shift is closed.

You just learned how to talk to the crew. That sounds small, but it's what separates "the model doesn't understand" from "the model delivers what you asked for."

**What you carry aboard:**

- **Anatomy and clarity.** The 4 layers of a prompt (role, context, examples, format), the difference between vague and specific, and how to close the doors a vague prompt leaves open.
- **Input shape.** XML tags as structural separators, the two channels (`system` vs `user`), and what belongs in each.
- **Examples as a technique.** Few-shot, how many to choose, how to cover the categories, and when to add counter-examples.
- **Asking for the output well.** Structured format, explicit nulls for missing data, real length constraints (characters, not words).
- **Reasoning and controlling the voice.** Chain of thought, decomposing big tasks, killing the preamble, and the reinforced-negative trap.
- **Debugging.** The loop of iterating a failing prompt, the mental catalog of the 5 typical failures.
- **Hardening for production.** Guardrails against dirty inputs, handling real and false refusals, resolving ambiguity before it bites.

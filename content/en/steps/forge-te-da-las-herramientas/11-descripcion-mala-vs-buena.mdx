        The agent invokes it reliably whenever an alias appears in the

        Real logs: 94% correct invocations, 0% wrong-context invocations.
    note: |
      The model isn't lazy: it's copying you. If your description says
      "does something with data", the invocation will be as ambiguous as
      that sentence. Every word in the description buys precision in the
      decision.

This step is the **heart of the Track**, condensed into a single comparison.

<Character name="Forge" /> already explained the principle in the previous step. Here you see it in data: same tool structure, two descriptions, two radically different production behaviors.

Toggle between the **Before** and **After** tabs. Notice three things that change:

- **The main verb** ("does something" vs "returns name, role, and current shift").
- **The use criterion** (absent vs "Use when the user mentions a crewmate by alias").
- **The anti-patterns** (absent vs "Do NOT use for crewmates referenced only by full name").

All three live in the description. None in the tool name.

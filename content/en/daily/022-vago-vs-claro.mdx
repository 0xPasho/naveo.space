title: "Quick mission: vague vs clear"
intro: |
  Same ask, two shapes. One gives the model something to grip, the other leaves it guessing. The whole difference is clarity.

scenes:
  - kind: prompt-AB
    question: |
      Atlas wants to thank the crew after closing a tough shift. Which
      of these two prompts will produce something usable?
    optionA: |
      ```
      Write me something nice.
      ```
    optionB: |
      ```
      Write a 3-line message to thank the crew after closing a tough
      shift. Warm tone, no empty phrases, mention that they held under
      pressure.
      ```
    correct: B
    explanation: |
      "Something nice" tells the model nothing. Nice for whom? Long or
      short? In what tone? You will get a generic text that could fit
      anyone, which means it fits no one.

      Option B pins three things: length (3 lines), audience (the crew
      after a tough shift), and tone (warm, no empty phrases). That
      trims the model's answer space and forces it to aim.

      Notice: clear is not longer for the sake of it, it is longer
      because it takes decisions off the model's plate.

      You have a vague prompt and want to make it clear. Order the

    steps:

  - kind: prompt-AB
    question: |
      You ask the model to write the same email in two different ways.
      Which one will produce a more predictable result?
    optionA: |
      ```
      Send me an email to tell the client the delivery is delayed.
      ```
    optionB: |
      ```
      Write an email telling the client the delivery is delayed by 48
      hours. Professional tone, max 4 lines, offer a 10% discount as
      compensation. Sign off with "Support Team".
      ```
    correct: B
    explanation: |
      The more specific, the more predictable the output. Option A can
      give you a 10-line email, or a one-liner, or one with a made-up
      discount, or no signature.

      Option B pins what matters: concrete reason (48 hours), tone,
      length (4 lines), a commercial gesture (10% discount), and a
      sign-off. Run it ten times and you will get ten close variants.

      Rule: every decision you do not make, the model will. Sometimes
      it makes them badly.
---

Vague = the model decides for you. Clear = you decide what should
come out. Take decisions off the model's plate and the results get
more consistent.
